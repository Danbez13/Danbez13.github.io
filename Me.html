What my best friends said about me:

Harrison:

Dan Bezant is a kind and gentle being. A big value of his, is usually to benefit others before himself. For example: He will check up on me during periods of time where we haven't been able to meet up as often. Asking how I have been and how I am doing, offering to get lunch, or just chill out together in order to relieve me of stress. However, Dan will avoid openly sharing his negative feelings. Not to say he is particularly self-destructive, but perhaps as a way to avoid gravitating his burden to others. He tends to rely souly on himself to deal with problems. Dan has fought educational systems for years with his dyslexia. Getting by seemingly okay, but by no means over-achieving to great lengths. For this reason, he developed a value most fail to recognise. A real value of people. There is nothing Dan would rather do,than to hang out with people. To be around them, to hear their stories and make new ones. He understands that time onearth is not an expectation, yet a gift. Too many examples of this exist in the 11 years I have known Dan. During intermediate school, nearly every friday night we spent at his place or mine playing video game. During College, having movie nights and (when we were old enough) driving around doing anything and everything. In our first year of Uni, he would come knocking on my dorm room door at some point during the day to hang out for an hour or so. Also, any social event Dan is invited to, he will attend. I can honestly say that the friendship I have with Dan exists more in the real world, than on a screen.

Kerri:

Overall, I believe Dan values his connections with people and he works hard to maintain close friendships
